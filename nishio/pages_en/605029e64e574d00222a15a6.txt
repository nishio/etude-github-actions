What is the minimum cut problem?
ã€€(This time we are only talking about directed graphs.)
ã€€Given a cut [$ (S, T)] of a directed graph [$ G = (V, E)], the edge [$ (u, v) \in E ] of G that is [$ u \in S, v \in T ] is called a cut edge.
ã€€[https://gyazo.com/359c47fe470936b043715e30d037e816]
ã€€The sum of the weights of the cut edges is called the size of the cut, and the cut with the smallest size is called the smallest cut.
ã€€Often it is convenient to consider "vertex s that always goes into S" and "vertex t that always goes into T." A cut such that s and t are so is sometimes called an "s-t cut.

ã€€Given a graph [$ G=(V,E)] with non-negative edge weights

ã€€ã€€[https://gyazo.com/8758e36faec9134793d689cb4a264558]

ã€€ã€€The weight of each side does not exceed the capacity at G: [$ F_{ij} \le E_{ij}]
