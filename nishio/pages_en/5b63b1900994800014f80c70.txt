Large Company Disease and Large Company Employee Disease
The "big company disease" seems to be a disease of big companies, but based on the principle of "[no company]," it is a disease of individual employees who work for big companies.

Not everyone is sick. Problems arise when the percentage of sick people increases or when sick people are in charge of decision making.

This disease can be transmitted by new employees. The more infectious [memes] are, the more widespread they are in the company.

 Excessive risk aversion
 Focus on measures that are likely to produce results (e.g., cost cutting)
 Disregard for outside knowledge
  [NIH Syndrome]
 When they think there is a problem, instead of reporting it, they talk about it behind your back.
