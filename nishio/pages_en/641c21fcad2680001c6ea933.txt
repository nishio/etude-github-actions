Sad Tropics
>The Sad Tropics (French: Tristes tropiques) is a book by anthropologist Claude [Lévi-Strauss], published in France in 1955, a travelogue documenting his travels among the minorities of [Brazil] in the 1930s. The ideas in the book, especially the excellent analysis of underdeveloped societies and the criticism of Eurocentrism, later earned the book sensational acclaim and made it one of the bibles of cultural anthropology and structuralism. It also had a profound influence on the humanities. In the last chapter of the book, there is a famous passage, "The world began without man, and it will end without man.
