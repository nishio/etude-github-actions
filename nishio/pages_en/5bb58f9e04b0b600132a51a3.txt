spontaneity and Maslow's stage theory of needs
Maslow's Theory of Needs is a hypothesis with no particular basis, but it is a useful framework.
Before hiring an employee, a company wants to know what kind of [desire for self-actualization] the employee has and whether he or she is willing to act on his or her own initiative.
On the other hand, those on the receiving end of employment interviews are placed in an anxious state of "I still haven't decided where to be hired.
Lower order needs are not being met.
Isn't it foolhardy to observe "what kind of higher-order desires we would have if they were fulfilled" in this state?
