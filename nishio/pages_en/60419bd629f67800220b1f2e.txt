You get something good that you didn't think you would get.
When you take various actions, those actions may bring "good results that you did not initially expect to get" ([unexpected success]).
People who have experienced this unexpected success will think that something good may come of it, and they will take action even if what they gain is unclear.
Those who have not experienced it consider cost-effectiveness based only on predictable outcomes. Since they are more reluctant to estimate effectiveness, they are more likely to choose not to act.
